Dhammapada: An Aphoristic Teaching


The Dhammapada is an ancient Pali text that all Buddhist sects recognize & is one the better known Buddhist scriptures. Though many sects don't use it as one of their main teachings, it is still has the upmost of reverence & is known as the original teachings of Siddartha Guatma(O.G. Buddha).

While aphorism is defined as:

  • A consise statement of a principle or truth
  • A minimal terse formulation of a truth or sentiment (truth can be individual!)

    • Okay, I get it, the whole aphorism thing might not be so clear, so here is a literal example of an aphoristic teaching from the Dhammapadda:

    “Hatred does not cease by hatred, but only by love; this is the eternal rule.”

    OR

    “Conquer the angry one by not getting angry; conquer the wicked by goodness; conquer the stingy by generosity, & the liar by speaking the truth.

    Finally, after the laman's explanation, I can get to the heart of why aphoristic teachings are so great. Because they are short, they are easy to remember. They often allude the main point of the verse as similar to something we underst& like: 'hatred is a fire which burns all it touches.'  Because of this, the Dhammapada is highly relatable & memorable. You could read the whole thing in one night, but spend your whole life dissecting & applying the underst&ings to your own experience. I found that this made underst&ings of the text itself to be a very deep knowledge that can be applied all areas of life.
